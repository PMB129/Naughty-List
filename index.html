More About Us

Naughty Dog

A flagship first-party studio inside PlayStation Studios and is one of the greatest video game development company in the industry. Naughty Dog is responsible for some of the most critically acclaimed and commercially successful games on Sony's PlayStation platforms, from the original Crash Bandicoot and Jak and Daxter series to modern franchises like Uncharted and The Last of Us.

Naughty Dog has garnered hundreds of industry and media awards and also gained a passionate fan base of millions of players around the globe for their use of cutting-edge technology and evocative, character-driven storytelling developments in their creations.

Naughty List's Choices

The Last Of Us

Set in the post-apocalyptic United States and tells the story of the survivors, Joel and Ellie, as they attempt to survive their westward journey across what remains of the country in search of a possible cure for the contemporary fungal epidemic that has nearly wiped-out humanity.

The Last Of Us II

Ellie and Joel have settled in Jackson, Wyoming, five years after their journey in the post-apocalyptic United States. Despite the continual threat of the infected and other, more desperate survivors, living amid a thriving community of survivors has provided them with tranquility and stability. When a horrific occurrence interrupts the peace, Ellie sets out on a never-ending quest for justice and closure.

Uncharted 4

THIEF'S END

Nathan Drake, now a retired fortune hunter, still feels the overwhelming lure of adventure. As he reunites with his long-lost brother, Samuel, promise of an unforgettable experience and undiscovered riches, it doesn’t take much to convince Nathan to pack his bags and set off to find the pirate utopia of Libertalia.

Uncharted 3

DRAKE'S DECEPTION

Set two years after, Among Thieves, Nathan Drake and his mentor Victor Sullivan tries to find the legendary Atlantis of the Sands, a lost city. While venturing their way to find the lost city, they were confronted by a mercenary group led by Sullivan’s former employer, Katherine Marlow.
